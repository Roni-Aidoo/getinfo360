A New Era: About Nana Yaa Akyaa II, the new Asantehemaa

Bismark Donkor Mensah July 13, 2026 2 min read Asante
Web

The Queen mothers in our traditional council marks a significant and unforgettable joy to many of her citizens Nana Ama Bonsu, officially enstooled as the 15th Asantehemaa for the Asante kingdom, succeeding the late Nana Konadu Yiadom lll, whose passing in 7th August, 2025 left the stool vacant. Nana Ama Bonusu emerges from the Royal family of the Oyoko of Manhyia reporting to be the granddaughter of the 13th Asantehene , Otumfuo Agyeman Prempeh l and a niece of the 14th Asantehene, Otumfuo Sir Osei Agyeman Prempeh ll, who still remains remarkably as one of the famous king in the Asante history. She was born on the 3rd June, 1946 to Daniel Kwame Bonah Sirbour & Odehyie Adwoa Akyaa. Her enstoolment provides a potential name as Nana Yaa Akyaa ll, reflecting and reviving the historic name of an earlier Asantehemaa who rule from 1884 to 1917 in the Asante history.

Her Educational Background

She had her basic education at Yaa Achiaa girls(1952 - 1957) , Mmofraturo Girls' Boarding School from 1958 and 1961 and had her Senior Secondry education at St. Louis Girls' Senior High School from 1961 to 1966. She is a sole proprietor withdiverse business operations

Her installation does not grant her the name as only Asantehemaa, but marks the beginning of new chapter in the leadership the Asante kingdom and preceding the centuries? old tradition of the Asante Queen Mothers. Nana Ama Bonsu has emerge as the pillar for promoting peace, unity, supporting projects in education, healthcare, girls’ education, women’s Empowerment and advising Asantehene on matters of governance and tradition of Asanteman which impact the nation positively.

Her Role As the Asantehemaa

Her potential responsibilities are remarkably going to be on the heart of Asanteman, thereby promoting change through dialogue and philosophy of non-violence to her citizens. Her occupant is regarded highly in the traditional office in the Asante kingdom. Her impactful responsibilities include

  • Advising the Asantehene on matters affecting the kingdom
  • Promoting peace, unity and reconciliation within the kingdom
  • Protecting the welfare of women, children, and vulnerable members of the society and Preserving Asante customs, traditions and cultural heritage
  • Serving as the female traditional leader of the Asante people

    • This is a representative of cultural preservation and socio- economic development, there uplifting the continued relevance of traditional leadership in modern Ghana, where chiefs and queen mothers collaborate with government institutions and development partners to improve their communities.
